APC Invoice Triage

Received an article-processing-charge invoice that feels off? Answer a short questionnaire about it. The tool returns a discrepancy register — each answer marked Consistent, Discrepancy, Common fraud pattern or Not answered, with one factual sentence each. It does not decide whether the invoice is genuine or whether you should pay it.

Next, regardless of the register above

  • Verify the journal's domain independently — search for the journal by name rather than clicking the invoice's link.
  • Contact the publisher using the email or contact address listed on the genuine journal website, not the address on the invoice.
  • Never pay a journal APC to a personal bank account or by cryptocurrency.
  • If anything above looks wrong, report it to your institution's research office or library before paying.

No overall verdict is calculated. A single "Common fraud pattern" answer can be decisive even when every other answer is Consistent. This register does not determine whether the invoice is genuine or whether you should pay it.

Method and limitations

Method version 1.0 · reviewed 20 July 2026. Four of your answers are checked against documented fraud patterns and simple factual comparisons (payment method, acceptance timing, payee-name match, domain match); a fifth, optional comparison checks your quoted amount against a fee OpenAlex records for the journal, if you look one up. Each check reports Consistent, Discrepancy, Common fraud pattern or Not answered with one sentence — never a combined score, percentage or overall verdict.

Not checked: whether the journal itself is legitimate (use the Journal Evidence Checker for that), the invoice's authenticity as a document, or anything about the underlying manuscript. An invoice preceding acceptance is treated as a fraud-pattern signal, but some fully open-access publishers legitimately disclose an upfront submission fee as stated policy — confirm this against the journal's own terms before relying on this signal alone. All answers stay in your browser; only an optional journal name/ISSN (for the APC lookup) is ever sent anywhere, and only to OpenAlex.
