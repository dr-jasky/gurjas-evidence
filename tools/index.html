Research Tools

Free, citation-ready instruments for serious researchers

Built by the Gurjas team on the methods we publish with. Free to use, no sign-up required, each grounded in the peer-reviewed literature it cites. Five tools process inputs in your browser. Journal Finder, Predatory Journal Checker and APC Checker send only the search term or ISSN to the named public databases when you press their action button; Gurjas does not receive those queries.

Live · Most used

Predatory Journal Risk Checker

Type a journal name or ISSN for a live check against OpenAlex, Crossref, DOAJ, the Scopus source list, the ABDC Journal Quality List and the Retraction Watch hijacked-journal register — plus a thirteen-signal red-flag assessment and a shareable evidence report. Reports signals; never defames.

Live · New

Journal Finder by Scope

Paste your abstract or keywords and see the journals that actually publish work like yours, ranked live from the open OpenAlex database — then verify each candidate before you submit.

Live · New

Should I Pay This APC?

Enter a journal name or ISSN to see its open-access status and any recorded article-processing charge from OpenAlex and DOAJ — and read whether a fee is a normal cost or a warning sign.

Live

SEM Sample Size Calculator

A-priori sample size for structural equation modeling: Westland's lower bound, Fisher-z power analysis and N:q heuristics — with a copy-ready, fully cited justification paragraph for your methods chapter.

Live

Reliability & Validity Quick Kit

Composite reliability and AVE from your standardised loadings, plus Cronbach's alpha from raw data — interpreted against Fornell & Larcker, Hair et al. and Nunnally thresholds.

Live

PhD Timeline Planner

Registration date in, milestone plan out — aligned to the UGC PhD Regulations 2022 timeframes, printable, with the honest sequencing most scholars discover too late.

Live

Grant Deadline Tracker

ICSSR, ANRF and allied funding calls for Indian researchers on one page, reviewed monthly, every row linked to its official source.

Live

NAAC Readiness Scorecard

Twenty questions mapped to the seven NAAC criteria — instant traffic-light scoring, criterion bars and your top five remediation priorities, printable for the IQAC file.

These tools support planning; they do not replace methodological judgement or formal advice. Suggestions welcome at support@gurjas.org.
