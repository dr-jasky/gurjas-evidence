Research Readiness Triage

Turn eight evidence-control checks into a gap register and next-control plan. Nothing is uploaded, no account is required, and no readiness score or automatic “go” decision is produced.

Step 1 · Set the decision context

What are you preparing to do?

Choose the closest pathway and current stage. The eight controls remain the same; the recommended route changes.

Step 2 · Record the evidence state

Eight controls before a consequential decision

Mark Evidenced only when the record exists and can be located. “Not applicable” is not a shortcut; use it only when you can explain why the control does not apply.

01 · Decision definition

The question, intended user and decision to be informed are written in one testable statement.

02 · Authority and source register

Current rules, definitions, literature or official sources are saved with access dates and ownership.

03 · Access, ethics and confidentiality

Data or record access, consent, ethics approval, confidentiality and permitted use are resolved.

04 · Operational definitions

Constructs, variables, indicators, evidence fields and exclusions are defined before interpretation.

05 · Design and coverage logic

Sampling, comparison, case selection or record-coverage logic—and likely blind spots—are documented.

06 · Analysis or synthesis plan

The analysis, verification or synthesis plan was written before inspecting the decisive result.

07 · Provenance and change control

Files, versions, transformations, decisions and responsible owners can be traced.

08 · Interpretation and decision boundary

Uncertainty, limitations, decision owner and the point for independent review are stated.

        Method and limitations

        Method version 1.1 · reviewed 20 July 2026. This is an unweighted Gurjas control inventory derived from the evidence lifecycle described on our Methods and Ethics Charter pages. It groups self-reported states into a gap register; it does not calculate readiness, risk probability or likely success.

        Not checked: whether an uploaded or claimed record exists, domain-specific completeness, legal or ethics approval, current regulatory requirements, statistical adequacy, evidence quality or decision-maker acceptance. “Not applicable” answers are listed separately in the copied record and must be justified outside this tool. All selections remain in your browser and are cleared when the page is closed or refreshed.
