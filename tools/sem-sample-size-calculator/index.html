How the three estimates work

The power-based minimum applies Cohen's (1988) framework through the Fisher-z transformation: the sample required to detect your anticipated effect size at the chosen power and significance level. Westland's (2010) lower bound reflects model structure — the ratio of observed indicators to latent variables — because complex measurement models need more information regardless of effect size. The N:q heuristic ties sample size to the approximate number of freely estimated parameters, following Jackson (2003) and Kline (2016); this calculator estimates q from your model dimensions. The recommended minimum is the largest of the three — the defensible choice under reviewer scrutiny.

This tool provides established a-priori conventions for planning, not a substitute for study-specific power simulation (e.g., Monte Carlo) where model particulars demand it. Need the full measurement model reviewed? That is what we do — start a conversation.
