# Session 4 — owner proof guide

The website must not publish invented clients, outcomes or testimonials. Please supply three real examples, one at a time, using the simple questions below. A case may be a Gurjas engagement or earlier professional experience led by the principal consultant, but earlier work will be labelled clearly as **principal experience before Gurjas**, not firm history.

## For each real example

1. What type of organisation was it? You may name it only when written permission exists.
2. Was the work completed by Gurjas, or by Dr. Jaskirat Singh before Gurjas was formed?
3. What month and year did the work begin and end?
4. What problem existed at the start?
5. What exact role was performed?
6. What method or analytical approach was used?
7. What was delivered?
8. What changed by the end? Give a number and timeframe where available. Otherwise provide a precise qualitative outcome.
9. What limitations, dependencies or boundaries should be stated?
10. May the organisation be named? If not, why is anonymisation necessary?
11. Where is the permission recorded: email, contract, message or signed note?
12. Is there an approved testimonial? Provide the exact approved wording and attribution level.
13. Is there a deliverable excerpt that may be published after redaction?

## Permission standard

- **Named case:** written permission must cover the organisation name, factual case wording, outcome and any logo or quote.
- **Anonymised case:** record why the identity is withheld and remove details that could re-identify the client.
- **Testimonial:** store the exact approved quote, attribution level, permission date and review/expiry date.
- **Sample deliverable:** remove names, contact details, identifiers, raw personal data, commercially sensitive values and hidden document metadata.

## What is already safe to publish

The editorial and corrections policy does not depend on client facts and may be published immediately. The case-study templates, evidence-library data and testimonial governance remain non-public until completed evidence passes automated checks.
