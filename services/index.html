Capabilities

Services

Six high-trust service lines for institutions, scholars and impact-oriented organisations. Every engagement is scoped in writing, delivered against documented methodology, and bounded by our ethics framework.

01 · Research Consulting

End-to-end support for the design and execution of serious research: conceptual framing, research design, methodology structuring, survey planning, sampling strategy, index development, evidence synthesis and analytical strategy. Typical clients include faculty teams preparing funded proposals, institutions commissioning studies, and organisations that need a research programme designed to publishable standards.

Typical deliverables: research design documents, instrument banks, sampling plans, analysis plans, annotated evidence syntheses and reviewer-ready methodology sections.

02 · Policy Evaluation

Assessment frameworks, evaluation logic, theory-of-change and impact-pathway mapping, field instruments and outcome-focused reporting for public programmes and institutional initiatives. We design evaluations that hold up to academic scrutiny while remaining readable by decision-makers.

Typical deliverables: evaluation frameworks, indicator matrices, baseline/endline designs, policy briefs and evidence memos.

03 · Impact Analytics

Quantitative analysis, qualitative synthesis, triangulation, dashboards, visual evidence and institutional impact reporting. We convert raw field data into defensible findings using the methods documented on our methodology page — SEM, fsQCA, econometric modelling and mixed-methods integration.

Typical deliverables: analysis reports, clean tables and figures, dashboards, decision briefs and replication-ready outputs.

04 · Institutional Advisory

NAAC/IQAC documentation support, research-capacity building, faculty development programme (FDP) design, publication mentoring and institutional research strategy for universities and colleges. We help institutions build genuine research capability — not paperwork theatre.

Typical deliverables: documentation audits, criterion-wise evidence structuring, research-policy drafts, FDP curricula and mentoring programmes.

05 · Doctoral Methodology Support

Technical, ethical and pedagogical support for doctoral researchers: research methodology, statistical modelling, analysis interpretation and viva-ready clarity. Our role is strictly advisory and educational — we teach, review and strengthen; we do not write theses or chapters. See our no-ghostwriting policy.

Typical deliverables: methodology consultations, model-specification reviews, output interpretation sessions and viva preparation.

06 · NGO & CSR Research

Needs assessment, impact studies, programme documentation, policy briefs, evidence reports and research-backed communication outputs for NGOs, foundations and corporate CSR teams that need credible, third-party evidence of programme effectiveness.

Typical deliverables: needs-assessment reports, impact evaluations, case documentation, donor-facing evidence summaries and annual impact reports.

Engagement model

Every engagement follows the same discipline: a written scope with objectives, timeline, data status and expected outputs; a methodology note recording analytical choices; staged delivery with review points; and confidential handling of all client materials. Fees are scoped per engagement — contact us with a brief description of your requirement to receive a proposal.

Illustrative scenarios

Evidence in action

How our methodology translates a research problem into decision-ready evidence — shown as three worked scenarios.

Institutional capacity · Higher education

Preparing an institution for accreditation

The challenge

A higher-education institution needs a comprehensive overhaul of its research documentation and capacity-building framework ahead of a NAAC/IQAC accreditation cycle.

Our method

Institutional advisory frameworks, mapping of faculty output against Scopus and Web of Science, and targeted faculty development programmes for research methodology.

The output

A fully structured, verifiable research dossier and an ongoing research-capacity pipeline built to survive rigorous institutional review.

Policy & welfare · Financial inclusion

Evaluating digital public infrastructure

The challenge

A policy actor needs to assess the downstream impact of digital public infrastructure and welfare delivery mechanisms on marginalised urban populations.

Our method

A quantitative analysis framework combined with qualitative synthesis, applying triangulation to evaluate the logic pathways of the financial intervention.

The output

A robust, evidence-led policy brief with clear impact metrics — limitations and assumptions stated — supporting governance reporting.

NGO / CSR impact · Development sector

Reconstructing impact without baseline data

The challenge

A regional NGO needs an independent impact study of a capability-based development programme to secure funding renewals, but lacks internal baseline data.

Our method

Retrospective evaluation logic to reconstruct impact pathways, needs assessments, and specialised indices to quantify capability improvements.

The output

A transparent, outcome-focused evidence report that translates on-ground development work into decision-ready analytics for CSR stakeholders.

These scenarios are illustrative of our methodology and typical outputs — they are not descriptions of specific clients or completed engagements. In line with our Ethics Charter, we only publish claims that can be verified.
