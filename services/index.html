Working practices

The questions serious teams ask first

Software, timelines, ethics approvals and the use of AI — answered upfront, so nothing surfaces mid-engagement.

SOFTWARE & PACKAGES

Your stack, not ours

We work in R, Python, Stata, SPSS/AMOS and SmartPLS, and deliver in the package your team already uses. If your researchers are committed to R, the entire engagement — code, outputs, replication files — is delivered in R.

TIMELINES

Your deadline sets the clock

Scoping starts by understanding your timeline, then we respond with what is realistically achievable. As a guide: enquiries answered within two working days; a methodology consult typically runs 1–3 weeks; a full evaluation 6–12 weeks. Indicative only — the agreed timeline is fixed in the written scope.

ETHICS & IRB

Approvals before fieldwork

For studies involving primary data from human participants, we ask about institutional ethics / IRB approval at scoping — and can support preparing the application. Where approval is required, fieldwork does not begin without it.

USE OF AI

Human-led, disclosed, never substitutive

AI tools may assist with bounded tasks — literature scanning, code drafting and editing, formatting. They never generate findings, analysis, citations or academic text for a client to submit as their own; every substantive output is produced and verified by the team, and client data is never entered into public AI systems. See our full AI transparency statement.

How engagements run

Five stages, no surprises

A controlled commercial pathway from first diagnostic to final handover. Select a stage to see what enters, what is agreed and what leaves it.

  1. 01DiagnosticAssess · diagnose
  2. 02ScopeDefine · bound
  3. 03AgreementConfirm · protect
  4. 04DeliveryExecute · review
  5. 05ReviewHandover · close
Stage 01 · Diagnostic

Establish the real starting point.

A structured, creditable review of where the engagement actually stands before a solution is proposed.

Input
Available materials, deadline and decision context
Decision control
Gaps, dependencies and material risks
Output
A credible starting-point review
Stage 02 · Scope

Turn the priority into a bounded brief.

The diagnostic is translated into written objectives, deliverables, timing and fee before substantive work begins.

Input
Diagnostic findings and the agreed priority
Decision control
Objectives, boundaries, assumptions and exclusions
Output
Written deliverables, timeline and fee
Stage 03 · Agreement

Make responsibilities explicit.

The accepted scope becomes a formal engagement with responsibilities, confidentiality and commercial milestones recorded.

Input
Accepted scope and authorised representatives
Decision control
Responsibilities, confidentiality and milestones
Output
Agreement and billing schedule
Stage 04 · Delivery

Work through documented review gates.

Delivery is staged so methods, evidence and emerging outputs can be examined at agreed points—not only at the end.

Input
Agreed data, materials and access
Decision control
Documented method and scheduled review points
Output
Staged, defensible deliverables
Stage 05 · Review

Close with an intelligible handover.

A final walkthrough records limitations, resolves handover questions and closes the information lifecycle responsibly.

Input
Final outputs and supporting materials
Decision control
Walkthrough, limitations and acceptance
Output
Handover plus confidential archive or return

These five stages govern the commercial side of an engagement; the research itself follows the four-phase engagement process described on our homepage. Common questions about pricing logic, authorship and turnaround are answered plainly on the FAQ page.
