Supporting capabilities

Broader capabilities behind the four offers

The six capability areas below remain available, but serious enquiries are routed through the four focused offers above.

01 · RESEARCH CONSULTING

Research designed to publishable standards

Conceptual framing, research design, methodology structuring, survey planning, sampling strategy, index development, evidence synthesis and analytical strategy — for faculty teams, institutions and organisations commissioning serious research.

Typical deliverables
  • Research design documents & instrument banks
  • Sampling and analysis plans
  • Reviewer-ready methodology sections
Typical engagement range
  • ₹15,000 – ₹1,00,000 — scaled to whether the design covers a single paper/chapter or a multi-study project.
02 · POLICY EVALUATION

Evaluations that hold up to scrutiny

Assessment frameworks, evaluation logic, theory-of-change and impact-pathway mapping, field instruments and outcome-focused reporting — rigorous enough for academia, readable by decision-makers.

Typical deliverables
  • Evaluation frameworks & indicator matrices
  • Baseline / endline designs
  • Policy briefs & evidence memos
Typical engagement range
  • ₹75,000 – ₹4,00,000 — driven mainly by fieldwork scale: a single-site evaluation sits at the lower end, a multi-region study with primary data collection at the higher end.
03 · IMPACT ANALYTICS

Raw field data → defensible findings

Quantitative analysis, qualitative synthesis, triangulation, dashboards and institutional impact reporting — using the methods on our methodology page: SEM, fsQCA, econometric modelling and mixed-methods integration.

Typical deliverables
  • Analysis reports, clean tables & figures
  • Dashboards & decision briefs
  • Replication-ready outputs
Typical engagement range
  • ₹20,000 – ₹1,50,000 — analysis-only work on data you already hold; the range reflects dataset size and model complexity (a single regression vs. SEM/fsQCA with multiple constructs).
04 · INSTITUTIONAL ADVISORY

Research capability, not paperwork theatre

NAAC/IQAC documentation support, research-capacity building, FDP design, publication mentoring and institutional research strategy for universities and colleges.

Typical deliverables
  • Documentation audits & criterion-wise evidence structuring
  • Research-policy drafts
  • FDP curricula & mentoring programmes
Typical engagement range
  • Documentation & readiness advisory — ₹1,00,000 – ₹2,50,000. Criterion-wise gap analysis, evidence structuring and DVV/DCF readiness review, without drafting the Self Study Report (SSR — the consolidated criterion-wise report an institution submits to NAAC) or attending the peer team visit.
  • Full SSR preparation + peer-visit support — ₹2,50,000 – ₹6,00,000. Everything above, plus drafting the SSR itself from the institution's own data across all criteria, extended profile compilation, mock assessment, and on-site support through the peer team visit.
05 · DOCTORAL METHODOLOGY SUPPORT

Viva-ready clarity — never substitution

Technical, ethical and pedagogical support for doctoral researchers: methodology, statistical modelling and interpretation. Strictly advisory and educational — see our no-ghostwriting policy.

Authorship stays with you. Your thesis and papers remain yours alone; our contribution is advisory and is acknowledged where appropriate — we do not claim co-authorship unless a separate research collaboration is agreed in writing.

Typical deliverables
  • Methodology consultations
  • Model-specification & output-interpretation reviews
  • Viva preparation sessions
Typical engagement range
  • Synopsis / proposal stage — ₹8,000 – ₹25,000. Research design validation, methodology and tool selection before you register your topic — a shorter, earlier-stage consultation.
  • Pre-submission / thesis-level review — ₹25,000 – ₹90,000. Statistical modelling and output-interpretation review across the full thesis, plus viva preparation — more sessions, later in the process.
06 · NGO & CSR RESEARCH

Third-party evidence funders can trust

Needs assessment, impact studies, programme documentation, policy briefs and evidence reports for NGOs, foundations and corporate CSR teams.

Typical deliverables
  • Needs-assessment & impact reports
  • Donor-facing evidence summaries
  • Annual impact reports
Typical engagement range
  • Small NGO / single-programme — ₹75,000 – ₹2,00,000. One site or programme, a needs assessment or a focused impact study with limited primary data collection.
  • Mid-sized CSR impact assessment — ₹2,00,000 – ₹5,00,000. Multi-site programmes, larger samples and the more rigorous design expected of a regulatory-grade CSR impact assessment.

The ranges above are a general guide, based on typical scope for each service line — not a price list. Every engagement follows the same discipline: a written scope with objectives, timeline, data status and expected outputs; a methodology note recording analytical choices; staged delivery with review points; and confidential handling of all client materials. Your exact fee is fixed in that written scope, not in the range — contact us with a brief description of your requirement and we'll tell you where it's likely to land.

Illustrative scenarios

Evidence in action

How our methodology translates a research problem into decision-ready evidence — shown as three worked scenarios.

Institutional capacity · Higher education

Preparing an institution for accreditation

The challenge

A higher-education institution needs a comprehensive overhaul of its research documentation and capacity-building framework ahead of a NAAC/IQAC accreditation cycle.

Our method

Institutional advisory frameworks, mapping of faculty output against Scopus and Web of Science, and targeted faculty development programmes for research methodology.

The output

A fully structured, verifiable research dossier and an ongoing research-capacity pipeline built to survive rigorous institutional review.

Policy & welfare · Financial inclusion

Evaluating digital public infrastructure

The challenge

A policy actor needs to assess the downstream impact of digital public infrastructure and welfare delivery mechanisms on marginalised urban populations.

Our method

A quantitative analysis framework combined with qualitative synthesis, applying triangulation to evaluate the logic pathways of the financial intervention.

The output

A robust, evidence-led policy brief with clear impact metrics — limitations and assumptions stated — supporting governance reporting.

NGO / CSR impact · Development sector

Reconstructing impact without baseline data

The challenge

A regional NGO needs an independent impact study of a capability-based development programme to secure funding renewals, but lacks internal baseline data.

Our method

Retrospective evaluation logic to reconstruct impact pathways, needs assessments, and specialised indices to quantify capability improvements.

The output

A transparent, outcome-focused evidence report that translates on-ground development work into decision-ready analytics for CSR stakeholders.

These scenarios are illustrative of our methodology and typical outputs — they are not descriptions of specific clients or completed engagements. In line with our Ethics Charter, we only publish claims that can be verified.

Working practices

The questions serious teams ask first

Software, timelines, ethics approvals and the use of AI — answered upfront, so nothing surfaces mid-engagement.

SOFTWARE & PACKAGES

Your stack, not ours

We work in R, Python, Stata, SPSS/AMOS and SmartPLS, and deliver in the package your team already uses. If your researchers are committed to R, the entire engagement — code, outputs, replication files — is delivered in R.

TIMELINES

Your deadline sets the clock

Scoping starts by understanding your timeline, then we respond with what is realistically achievable. As a guide: enquiries answered within two working days; a methodology consult typically runs 1–3 weeks; a full evaluation 6–12 weeks. Indicative only — the agreed timeline is fixed in the written scope.

ETHICS & IRB

Approvals before fieldwork

For studies involving primary data from human participants, we ask about institutional ethics / IRB approval at scoping — and can support preparing the application. Where approval is required, fieldwork does not begin without it.

USE OF AI

Assistive, never generative

AI tools may assist with internal drafting and code checks. They never generate findings, analysis or citations; every output is human-verified against source data, and client data is never entered into public AI systems.
