Editorial and corrections policy

Gurjas publishes research guidance, policy analysis, tools, service information and business proof. This policy explains how claims are sourced, reviewed, corrected and kept distinct from marketing assertion.

Correction contact: support@gurjas.org. Please include the page URL, disputed wording and supporting source.

Responsibility

Named ownership for every published claim

AUTHORSHIP

Who is responsible

Pages identify the responsible organisation or named expert where appropriate. A listed author remains accountable for the accuracy, interpretation and limitations of the published material.

EXPERT REVIEW

Specialist claims

Technical, methodological and first-person professional claims require review by the relevant named expert before publication. Editorial staff do not infer experience, permissions or outcomes from academic credentials.

BUSINESS PROOF

Cases and testimonials

Client cases, quotes and sample deliverables are published only after factual review and recorded permission or a documented anonymisation rationale. Earlier professional work is labelled as principal experience before Gurjas, not firm history.

Evidence standard

Source hierarchy and claim discipline

SOURCE HIERARCHY

Prefer the closest authoritative source

  1. Primary laws, regulations, official notices, standards and first-party datasets.
  2. Original research papers, institutional reports and publisher records.
  3. High-quality reviews or professional guidance when primary material needs interpretation.
  4. Secondary commentary only when its role and limitations are clear.
CLAIM CONTROLS

What must be visible

  • Current or regulated claims cite dated primary sources.
  • Quantitative outcomes state a baseline, timeframe or explicit qualitative framing.
  • Uncertainty, assumptions and limitations are not removed to strengthen a headline.
  • Illustrative scenarios are labelled and are never presented as completed client work.
Human accountability

Use of AI and automated tools

ASSISTIVE USE

Drafting and technical checks

AI or automated tools may assist with structure, language refinement, code checking or internal quality control. They do not replace source verification, expert judgement or authorship responsibility.

PROHIBITED USE

No fabricated evidence

AI-generated citations, findings, client histories, testimonials, permissions or professional experience are not acceptable. Sensitive client material is not entered into public AI systems. Material use of automation is disclosed where it affects how a reader should interpret the work.

Maintenance

Review, updates and corrections

REVIEW DATES

Visible freshness

Material guidance should show a reviewed or updated date. Current policy, regulation, pricing, metrics and tool-source statements are reviewed when the underlying source or operating practice changes.

CORRECTIONS

Prompt and proportionate

Clear factual errors are corrected as soon as reasonably practicable. Material changes should update the visible review date and, where useful, explain what changed. Minor spelling or formatting fixes may be made without a change note.

DISAGREEMENTS

Evidence over preference

Requests are assessed against sources and context. Gurjas may retain a supported interpretation while clarifying uncertainty or linking a credible alternative view. Corrections are not exchanged for payment, favourable coverage or removal of legitimate criticism.

Independence

Conflicts, commercial relationships and confidentiality

CONFLICTS

Disclose what may affect interpretation

Relevant funding, advisory roles, commercial relationships or other interests should be disclosed when they could reasonably affect how a reader interprets a publication, review or recommendation.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Permission does not remove restraint

Even with publication permission, case studies and samples disclose only what is necessary to evidence the work. Personal data, confidential methods, raw datasets, security details and commercially sensitive information remain excluded unless lawful, necessary and explicitly authorised.

Last reviewed: 14 July 2026. This policy is reviewed after a material correction incident, a change in editorial workflow or at least annually.
