# Library Outreach Plan (internal — not published)

Goal: get gurjas.org's free tools (Predatory Journal Checker, Journal Finder, APC Checker)
linked from university library research guides. These are high-authority `.edu`/`.ac.in`
backlinks, and — more importantly — they put the Checker in front of exactly the audience
it's built for, at the moment they need it.

## Why this works
Academic librarians who maintain "avoid predatory journals" guides are actively looking for
good resources to link — that's the whole point of the guide. We're not asking for a favour;
we're offering a free, no-login, no-signup tool their patrons can use immediately. This is a
normal, welcomed kind of pitch in the library world.

## How to find the right person (don't email the general enquiries inbox)
1. Search `site:<university-domain> "subject librarian"` or `"scholarly communication librarian"`.
2. On LibGuides pages (the ones above), the guide's owner is usually named at the bottom
   ("Guide created by...") with an email or profile link — email that person directly.
3. For Indian institutions without a named guide, the university library's "Research Support"
   or "Reference Services" page usually lists a subject librarian per department — for the
   research-integrity angle, target Library Science / Information Science or the general
   Reference/Research Support head.

## Cadence
- Send in small batches (5-8 a week), not a mass blast — each email should read as personally
  sent, referencing the specific guide/page you found.
- No follow-up needed if no reply — librarians are busy; one email, no pressure, is the right
  tone for this kind of ask.
- Track responses in a simple sheet: institution, contact, date sent, reply, outcome (linked /
  no reply / declined) — mainly so you don't accidentally email the same person twice.

## What NOT to do
- Don't pitch DOAJ, COPE, OASPA or Think.Check.Submit for a "listing" — none of them run a
  self-service submission process for third-party tools; they're citation/reference sources
  for your content, not outreach targets.
- Don't mass-email from a script or send identical text to many institutions in one send —
  it reads as spam and damages the exact trust this outreach is meant to build.
